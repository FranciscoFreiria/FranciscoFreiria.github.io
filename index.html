Master Thesis

Kidney segmentation in MRI images using deep learning: A comparison between U-NET and Attention U-NET

My thesis addresses the increasing prevalence of Chronic Kidney Disease, which affects over 10% of the global population.
I focused on deep learning methods for kidney segmentation, an essential task in monitoring disease progression, particularly in patients with Autosomal Dominant Polycystic Kidney Disease. Using U-Net and Attention U-Net architectures, I applied both 2-class and 3-class segmentation to improve the segmentation of border regions. By experimenting with cross-entropy and focal loss functions on the PARENCHIMA dataset, I achieved a Dice coefficient of 0.966%, showcasing the potential for more accurate, user-independent kidney volume assessments that could reduce the need for manual segmentation and invasive biopsies.
